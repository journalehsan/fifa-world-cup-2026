Format & expansion

Starting in 2026, the World Cup expands from 32 to 48 teams. Teams are divided into 12 groups of four. The top two in each group, plus the eight best third-placed teams, advance to a new round of 32. This is the first major format change since 1998.

The total number of matches rises from 64 to 104, and the tournament length increases to 39 days. Teams reaching the final four will play eight matches instead of seven, but each team still plays three group-stage games.

Slot allocation & qualifiers

  • Hosts Canada, Mexico and the United States automatically qualified.
  • All six confederations now have at least one guaranteed spot.
  • For the first time, OFC has a guaranteed berth.
  • An inter-confederation playoff with six teams decides the final two spots.

Draw & seeding overview

The final draw took place on December 5, 2025, at the Kennedy Center in Washington, D.C. The 48 teams were divided into four pots of 12 based on the November 2025 FIFA rankings, with hosts pre-allocated:

  • Mexico in Group A, opening match vs South Africa at Estadio Azteca.
  • Canada in Group B, opening in Toronto.
  • United States in Group D, opening in Inglewood.

The official theme is an instrumental track titled "FIFA World Cup 26 Theme Song". In 2025, 16 remixes were released, each produced by artists connected to a host city (for example DJ Jazzy Jeff for Philadelphia and Tech N9ne for Kansas City).

Tickets & hospitality

Ticket prices for the 2026 World Cup start around $60 for group stage matches and can exceed $6,700 for the final, a significant increase compared to 2022. For the first time, FIFA is using dynamic pricing, so prices can move up or down depending on demand.

  • Sales phased in multiple windows (including Visa-only priority phases).
  • Maximum of four tickets per person per match.
  • Maximum of 40 tickets per person across the whole tournament.
  • FIFA plans an official resale platform for fans.

Note: This is an unofficial fan site. Always check the official FIFA ticketing platform for real prices, dates and availability.

Controversies & concerns

Like recent World Cups, the 2026 edition has raised questions around climate, labor and human rights. This site summarizes what has been reported; it is not an official FIFA position.

Climate & heat

  • Researchers warn about high summer wet-bulb temperatures in several host cities.
  • Concerns over increased air travel between 16 cities and overall carbon footprint.
  • FIFA has introduced mandatory three-minute hydration breaks per half, which can also be used for commercials.

Labor & human rights

  • Union groups raised concerns about working conditions at Mexico's Estadio Azteca renovations.
  • Human rights organizations have called on FIFA and host governments to safeguard protest rights, media freedom and LGBTQ+ rights.
  • U.S. immigration and travel-ban policies have triggered debate about fan access from some countries.
